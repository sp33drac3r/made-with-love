About

My whole life I’ve been captivated by new products. I was a child of the information age and growing up felt like everyday was a new and exciting future. When I was maybe 8 my dad brought home a Zip drive for work. He told me that a single Zip disk could fit the complete works of Shakespear. Today the complete works of Shakespeare and fit in a tweet?! A few years later, I got my first MP3 player.

I was an avid reader of Popular Science magazine. I remember one cover that predicted the next big thing: the all in one device. A one stop shop MP3 player, phone, palm pilot. When Steve Jobs announced the iPhone many years later I thought, “here it is. The future is now.”

I didn’t have much of a method when I first started noodling on new product ideas. Inspired by the combination mp3 player / phone idea I thought, “what other combo products should there be?” To my parents' horror, I once explained in great detail over dinner about an idea I had for a Lay-Z-Boy / toilet. Kick up your feet, flip on the game and take a nice, comfortable dump. My trash heap of discarded ideas is deep and diverse but every idea in it has one thing in common - I thought it was a winner at the time.

And so too many of the ideas I share here will one day look stupid. But that’s OK. These product ideas keep building up in my head and I need a place to put them. The ideas themselves have no real value because most of them aren’t great. And more and more I realize that I’ll only ever be able to chase after a precious few. Rarely does one meet the requirements of being a problem that I have the expertise to solve, capital to move forward and time to allocate toward it’s development.

So, here you go. Here are some ideas. Make of them what you will.
